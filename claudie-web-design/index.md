<h4><a href="http://saguenay-youth-study.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Saguenay Youth Study</a></h4>
<p><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-588" src="claudie-web-design/images/Saguenay-Youth-Study.jpg" alt="Saguenay Youth Study" width="975" height="575" srcset="claudie-web-design/images/Saguenay-Youth-Study.jpg 975w, claudie-web-design/images/Saguenay-Youth-Study-768x453.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 975px) 100vw, 975px" /></p>
<p>The <strong>Saguenay Youth Study (SYS)</strong> is carried out in a population with a known <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Founder_effect" target="_blank" rel="noopener">founder effect</a>, namely in French Canadians living in the Saguenay Lac Saint-Jean region of Quebec, Canada.</p>
<p>The Saguenay Youth Study (SYS) is a two-generational study of adolescents and their parents (n=1,029 adolescents and 962 parents) aimed at investigating trajectories of cardio-metabolic and brain health. The ultimate goal of this study is to identify effective means for increasing healthy life expectancy.</p>
