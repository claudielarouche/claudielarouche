Challenge Number 6: Eat fruits every day between breakfast and lunch
You don’t actually have to redo your fruit bowl for this challenge but it will certainly help in order to complete it! Eating fruits can be done anytime throughout the day (and multiple times too!) but I think it increases the chances that you’ll actually do it if you have a specific moment during the day when you’re planning on having your fruit. If you’re interested in making this a new habit, I suggest you attach it to an existing habit of yours (for example eating fruits while reading the morning newspaper, or eating fruits after you’ve finished your coffee, or eating fruit after your daily 10 o’clock meeting, you get the idea). 

Canada’s Food Guide has lots of suggestions on how to incorporate more fruits to your diet. Here are few things I personally do but of course this is what works for me, not necessarily the best for you:
 
  • I buy a big box of frozen blueberries from Farm Boy (5 lb) and add them to my sugar-free yogourt (they make sugar-free yogourt taste a lot better!) I like that the packaging is made of paper so it can be recycled. As for the sugar-free yogourt, I buy the Pinehedge organic yogourt that comes in a glass container that can be returned.  There are also delicious vegan alternatives for yogourt for those of you who would like to add a bit of adventure to the experience. As a start, try the Silk brand made of coconut milk.
  • When they are in season, I buy 3 pomegranates that I prep on the weekend and slowly eat during the week (the pomegranate seeds keep just fine in the fridge for 7 days). On a side note, this year someone made me discover a new way of seeding pomegranates that works a LOT better than soaking them in water. You open it in half and smack it with a wooden spoon on top of a bowl, and the seeds just nicely fall off. See this video if you don’t believe me. 
  • I buy cantaloupes regularly, cut it up over the weekend and slowly eat it during the week
